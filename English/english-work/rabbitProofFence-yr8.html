INTRODUCTION

The movie: Rabbit-Proof Fence examines belonging and identity in the First Nations People through the experiences of the Stolen Generation. it follows the story of three girls from said Stolen Generation torn away from their family. The main character Molly escapes Moore River with her sister, Daisy and cousin, Gracie, to return home to her family. Noyce shows the traumatic effect on the chilldren and families that were stolen.

PART 1

The Stolen Generation is First Nations People that were forcibly taken away from their parents, homes and lands. the trauma in the scene was shown when the girls were taken away from their family. there was alot of screaming and movement used in the scene to demonstrate how stressful and devastated the characters felt. an intimate close-up shot framed the three girls faces looking out the back window of the police car. it was used to convey sadness and dispare as they were taken from where they belong.
